### What our draft covers that competitors do NOT
The Gianna draft has 5 clear differentiators against every top-10 competitor we audited:

1. **The 60% HSG false-positive statistic, prominently placed.** No competitor leads with this. Cleveland Clinic, Veritas, Loma Linda, MedicalNewsToday all assume the HSG result is real. This is the single most powerful AEO and CTR hook on the entire page.
2. **The 3-segment pathway framing** (proximal / mid / distal -> FTR / anastomosis / neosalpingostomy) as a structured *patient decision tool*. 3. **"IVF bypasses the tube without treating it"** as an explicit clinical argument. Every IVF-clinic competitor positions surgery as a failed attempt that leads to IVF. Our draft positions IVF as a failure mode that skips diagnosis. This is the editorial wedge.

## 5. Title Tag Variants

Each option is annotated with the keyword it prioritizes, character count, and a CTR hypothesis. Hard SERP truncation: 60 chars (Google), 65 chars (some carrier-side rendering).

### Option A -- "Blocked Tubes on HSG: 3 Restorative Options Before IVF" (52 chars) [SEO REVIEW DEFAULT]
- **Primary keyword:** `blocked tubes` (front-loaded)
- **Hook:** "3 Restorative Options" promises a listicle and signals structured answer (AI Overview food)
- **Differentiator:** "Before IVF" -- explicitly contrarian, sets up the editorial wedge in the SERP
- **CTR hypothesis:** Wins against generic clinic titles ("Blocked Fallopian Tubes - Symptoms, Causes, Treatment") for decision-stage searchers (people who already know they have a blockage and are evaluating options). Loses some symptom-stage searchers who don't yet know they have a blockage and aren't yet thinking about IVF.

### Option B -- "Blocked Fallopian Tubes: What HSG Misses (and 3 Fixes)" (57 chars)
- **Primary keyword:** `blocked fallopian tubes` (the higher-MSV head form)
- **Hook:** "What HSG Misses" is curiosity-gap-style, hints at the 60% statistic
- **Differentiator:** "3 Fixes" -- concrete, numeric, downstream of curiosity
- **CTR hypothesis:** Stronger curiosity hook than Option A. May get higher CTR but slightly less qualified (people clicking through curiosity rather than decision intent). Better for top-of-funnel awareness; weaker for converting to a clinician inquiry.

### Option C -- "Can Blocked Fallopian Tubes Be Fixed Without IVF?" (49 chars)
- **Primary keyword:** Question-format match to the FAQ Q1 (`can blocked fallopian tubes be unblocked without ivf`)
- **Hook:** Direct AEO match -- this title is the literal AI query
- **Differentiator:** Question framing reads naturally in voice-search and Bing AI Overviews
- **CTR hypothesis:** Highest AEO citation potential of the three. Slightly lower SERP CTR because question titles can read as "uncertain" to skim-readers, but voice-first / AI surfaces will love it. AI Overview pickup is a strong tradeoff.

### Option D -- "Blocked Tubes: HSG, FTR, and the IVF Alternative" (50 chars)
- **Primary keyword:** `blocked tubes` (front), plus 3 secondary acronyms (`HSG`, `FTR`)
- **Hook:** Lists the medical entities by name -- signals clinical depth
- **Differentiator:** "IVF Alternative" -- captures the `ivf alternative blocked tubes` long-tail directly
- **CTR hypothesis:** Strong for clinical-savvy searchers (someone who has been told they need IVF and is researching alternatives). Weaker for first-time HSG patients who don't know what FTR means. Conversion-stage rather than awareness-stage.

### Recommendation
**Use Option A as the title tag.** It is the most balanced across SERP CTR, AEO retrieval, decision-stage intent, and primary-keyword position. It is also the title the SEO review already validated.

Hold Option C ("Can Blocked Fallopian Tubes Be Fixed Without IVF?") as the H1 alternative if Brian wants to lean further into AEO pickup. The Gianna draft's current H1 ("Your HSG Says Your Tubes Are Blocked. Your Doctor Says IVF. Here Is What You Were Not Told.") is editorially excellent but does not contain the primary keyword and only matches the FAQ-style query pattern weakly. The optimal pairing is:

- **Title (SEO):** "Blocked Tubes on HSG: 3 Restorative Options Before IVF"
- **H1 (CTR / emotional):** Keep the current Gianna H1 as written (it does its job on post-click engagement)
- **Meta description:** Keep current draft, lightly tightened per SEO review §5 ("Blocked fallopian tubes on HSG do not mean IVF is the only option. Three restorative procedures, FTR, anastomosis, neosalpingostomy -- and why 60% of HSG blockages aren't real.")

This split (SEO title != H1) is standard practice and supported by Astro's BaseLayout title/H1 separation.
