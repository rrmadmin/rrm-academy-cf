Do Blocked Tubes Have Symptoms?

Many women with blocked fallopian tubes have no symptoms at all and only learn of the diagnosis during a fertility workup. When symptoms do appear, they are most often pelvic pain, painful periods, or unusual vaginal discharge — the last is particularly associated with hydrosalpinx, where a fluid-filled tube is the source. There is no reliable home test. The diagnostic gateway is almost always the same: a hysterosalpingogram, or HSG.
