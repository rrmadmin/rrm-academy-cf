What is Restorative Reproductive Medicine?

Restorative Reproductive Medicine (RRM) is an approach within conventional medicine that works cooperatively with the body. It uses standard and advanced diagnostic tools to investigate the fundamental “why” behind symptoms. By identifying and treating the underlying cause, whether hormonal, anatomical, or inflammatory, we can restore natural function and optimize health.

While our work is primarily focused on women, the RRM model also provides powerful solutions for men seeking to improve their reproductive health. This is not about symptom suppression; it’s about authentic healing.

The RRM Approach

  1. Identify the cause. Your body’s signals, the painful periods, the irregular cycles, are clues that have likely been ignored. We teach you how to chart your cycle, turning your experiences into valuable data. For a NaPro-trained OB/GYN, that chart is like an EKG for a cardiologist; it helps uncover the root cause of your health issues.
  2. Treat the condition. Once we know the why, we can discover targeted medical and surgical treatments that address the underlying disease. If you have endometriosis, the goal isn’t just to manage the pain; it’s to skillfully resect the disease itself.
  3. Restore normal function. The ultimate goal is to restore your body’s natural hormonal balance and reproductive function. A healthy system is a fertile system. This approach can lead to improved fertility and healthier pregnancies because we’ve fixed the broken pipe instead of just using buckets to catch the leak.
  4. Cooperate with the body. RRM works in harmony with your natural cycle. You will learn to understand and appreciate your body’s intricate design, becoming an active, empowered partner in your own healthcare journey.

Programs & Services

RRM Academy bridges the gap between complex medical science and the people who need it most:

  • Clinician-Led Courses: Developed and taught by medical experts, translating years of clinical experience into accessible, self-paced online education with video lectures, downloadable resources, and transcripts.
  • Patient Empowerment: Equipping individuals and couples with the knowledge to understand their bodies, ask informed questions, and advocate for better care.
  • Professional Training: Resources for healthcare providers who want to integrate restorative principles into their own practices.
  • Evidence-Based Resources: From in-depth masterclasses to an extensive research library, all content is grounded in scientific evidence and clinical best practices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is RRM evidence-based?
Yes. RRM is grounded in conventional medical science. It uses standard and advanced diagnostic tools, including hormonal panels, imaging, and surgical evaluation, to identify root causes. The RRM Research Library contains over 3,100 peer-reviewed publications supporting this approach.
Is RRM the same as natural family planning?
No. While RRM uses cycle charting as a diagnostic tool (similar to how a cardiologist reads an EKG), it is a full medical and surgical discipline. RRM includes hormone therapy, minimally invasive surgery, and other conventional treatments, all aimed at restoring the body’s natural function.
How does RRM differ from IVF?
IVF bypasses the reproductive system to achieve pregnancy. RRM investigates why conception isn’t happening, treats the underlying condition, and restores normal fertility. Published data shows RRM achieves a 32.1% adjusted live birth rate across all age groups, and 27% for women over 40, compared to IVF’s 9% in that age group.
Do I need to be religious to benefit from RRM?
No. While NaProTECHNOLOGY (a specific type of RRM) was developed at a Catholic institution, the approach is entirely scientific and medical. RRM Academy serves patients and professionals of all backgrounds.
What is NaProTECHNOLOGY?
NaProTECHNOLOGY (Natural Procreative Technology) is a specific and highly effective form of RRM developed by Dr. Thomas Hilgers. It combines standardized cycle charting with targeted medical and surgical interventions to diagnose and treat reproductive health conditions at their source.
