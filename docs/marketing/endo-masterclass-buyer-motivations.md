# Endo Masterclass: Buyer Motivations

Pre-course intake survey responses from 54 paying students (January 2023 onward). These are real answers from real buyers explaining why they purchased the Masterclass in Endometriosis & Surgery. Test entries excluded. Data from two sources: Teachable pre-class questionnaire (38 students with usernames) and Google Forms (16 students with emails only, no names).

## Audience breakdown (from self-identification, where provided)

- **~32** patients ("Endo Warriors")
- **~8** clinicians/educators ("I care for women with endometriosis")
- **~10** Both (patients who are also healthcare providers)
- **~4** unclassified (Google Forms respondents who didn't fill self-description, inferred from responses)

This mixed audience is a defining feature: patients and providers sit in the same classroom.

## Key themes

1. **"How do I pick the right surgeon?"** -- The single most common motivation. Appears in 13+ responses.
2. **Self-advocacy and empowerment** -- "educated and empowered," "advocate for myself," "confidence," "the right questions"
3. **Is surgery right for me?** -- Many are pre-surgical and terrified. They need information to make the decision.
4. **Mixed audience** -- Patients and providers in the same room. Several are both simultaneously.
5. **Knowledge gap, not motivation gap** -- These buyers are already motivated. They arrive wanting NaPro. They just don't know enough to act.
6. **International reach** -- Students from Canada, Australia seeking access to NaPro-quality care
7. **Post-surgical questions** -- Some have already had surgery and want to know about re-growth, maintenance, long-term management

## Takeaways for marketing

- **The #1 selling point is surgeon selection**, not disease education. "How to pick a surgeon" appears more than any other phrase.
- Buyers arrive already motivated but under-informed. They score high on wanting a NaPro surgeon (4.2) but low on knowing what to look for (adhesion prevention 1.2, electrocautery comfort 1.0, screening questions 1.9).
- The strongest pitch is: "Learn what your doctor should be doing and isn't."
- Several buyers are clinicians seeking knowledge they were never taught in training. The course works for both audiences.
- "Advocacy," "empowerment," and "the right questions" appear more than any clinical term.
- Comfort going to ANY OB/GYN without researching = 1.1. These patients fundamentally do not trust the system. The course builds informed trust, not blind trust.
- International students are buying despite geographic barriers to NaPro access. The education itself has standalone value.
