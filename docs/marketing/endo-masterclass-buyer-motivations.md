# Endo Masterclass: Buyer Motivations

Pre-course intake survey responses from paying students (January 2023). These are real answers from real buyers explaining why they purchased the Masterclass in Endometriosis & Surgery.

## What they hoped to gain

- "Insight as to why I have endometriosis and what caused it to progress"
- "Better ways of coping with endo symptoms and potentially learning ways to slow growth"
- "Understand more about what causes endometriosis, how it affects fertility, treatment options, different surgery techniques and benefits"
- "Current research on surgical outcomes, recurrence rates and tips and tricks"
- "Clarity if the path to surgery is correct for my situation"
- "How to find a good surgeon"
- "Management strategies for endo, and more knowledge to be a better advocate"
- "I want to be able to ask the right questions that will help me and others discover how best to advocate for ourselves when seeking quality health care"
- "I expect to learn unconventional knowledge about endometriosis and women's healthcare not received from other doctors"
- "Hope to gain knowledge about new topics of endometriosis I didn't know and reinforce things I already knew with scientific evidence through the scope of restorative fertility practices"
- "Knowledge and guidance I can share with my patients in my non surgical practice"
- "Knowledge to help me assess which surgeon to trust with my own potential surgery, how to manage/care for self in preparation of surgery"
- "How to manage care / navigate between providers for best endometriosis/fertility care"
- "Learn more about how I can navigate the health system as a patient with endometriosis (I am also a healthcare provider)"
- "Learn how to better assist endometriosis patients (including myself) as a fertility doula"

## Problems they were trying to solve

- Infertility (multiple students)
- Understanding endometriosis in fertility challenges
- General lack of information and knowledge about the disease
- Women choosing suboptimal surgical options for their endometriosis
- Navigating between providers for best endo/fertility care

## Key themes

1. **Self-advocacy**: Knowing what questions to ask, screening surgeons, being a better advocate
2. **Understanding the disease**: Causes, progression, why it happened, what it looks like
3. **Navigating the medical system**: Finding good surgeons, managing between providers, knowing who to trust
4. **Making informed decisions**: Is surgery right for me? What are my real options?
5. **Mixed audience**: Both patients AND healthcare providers (doulas, NPs, non-surgical physicians) bought the course

## Pre-course confidence (0-5 scale, averaged across 10 respondents)

| Skill | Pre-course avg |
|-------|---------------|
| Speaking to a surgeon about endometriosis | 3.7 |
| Knowing what questions to ask to screen a surgeon | 3.1 |
| Making a decision whether surgery is right for you | 3.5 |
| Describing what endometriosis is | 3.9 |
| Describing different appearances of endometriosis | 1.9 |
| Understanding the purpose of diagnostic surgery | 3.6 |
| Understanding theories of endometriosis origin | 1.7 |
| Understanding basics of adhesion prevention | 1.5 |
| Comfort with Gortex membrane placement | 1.3 |
| Likelihood to choose a NaPro trained surgeon | 5.0 |
| Comfort going to any OB/GYN without researching | 1.4 |
| Comfort with surgeon using electrocautery | 0.9 |
| Comfort advising patients/friends about endo surgery | 3.7 |

## Takeaways for marketing

- Buyers arrive already motivated but under-informed. They score high on wanting a NaPro surgeon (5.0) but low on knowing what to look for (adhesion prevention 1.5, electrocautery comfort 0.9).
- The strongest selling point is not "learn about endo" but "learn what your doctor should be doing and isn't."
- Several buyers are clinicians themselves seeking knowledge they were never taught in training.
- "Advocacy" and "the right questions" appear more than any clinical term.
