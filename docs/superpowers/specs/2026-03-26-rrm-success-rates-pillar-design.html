Purpose

A single authoritative page synthesizing all published RRM outcomes data. Replaces the need to visit IIRRM's flat bullet list. Answers the question patients actually ask: "Does this work?"

Audience

Primary: The Exhausted Patient 30-42 failed conventional treatment trust damaged and The Values-Driven Couple 26-40 decided against IVF

Secondary: The Curious Generalist (OBGYN/family medicine) and The Frustrated OBGYN (wants outcome data to cite)

VOC constraints:

  • Empathy before proof. Recognition before statistics.
  • OB/GYN comfort score is 1.1/5. These patients have earned their skepticism.
  • Never position against IVF combatively. Frame as "a different approach to a different question."
  • Buyers are already motivated. They lack information, not motivation.

Page Structure

1. Recognition Section (above the fold)

Short, empathetic opening. Validates the reader's experience before any data. Per VOC: "What I was told would work, didn't" is the shared origin story.

H1: "RRM Success Rates: What the Research Shows"

1-2 sentences acknowledging the search. Bridge to evidence. No stats in this section. Recognition first.

2. Key Takeaways Box

Styled callout (same pattern as /what-is-rrm/). 4-5 bold lead statements with "RRM" as subject (AEO-optimized):

  • RRM research reports live birth rates of 32% to 66% depending on patient population and follow-up duration.
  • RRM has been studied in over 4,500 couples across 8 countries on 4 continents.
  • RRM achieves pregnancies even after failed IVF. 403 couples, 100% prior IVF failure, 32.1% adj. cum. LBR, 92% full-term.
  • RRM reduces "unexplained" infertility to under 10% of cases. Idiopathic infertility dropped from 30-50% to 8%.
  • RRM births are overwhelmingly singleton and full-term. Multiple pregnancy rates near zero.

3. The Evidence: RRM Outcomes by Study

Narrative synthesis organized by theme, not chronologically. Each subsection weaves 2-3 studies.

  • 3a: "Does RRM work for infertility?" -- Core outcomes. Sanchez-Mendez, JABFM, Canada.
  • 3b: "What if I've already tried IVF?" -- de Groot/Boyle 2018, Andrology prior-ART subgroup.
  • 3c: "Does age matter?" -- Age-stratified data. Honest about constraints.
  • 3d: "What about male factor infertility?" -- Grande/Andrology 2025. 87% treatable causes.
  • 3e: "How long does it take?" -- Time-to-conception data vs IVF timelines.

4. Study Comparison Table

Interactive/sortable table for clinicians. All 10 outcomes studies. Columns: Study, Year, Country, N, Mean age, Prior ART %, Duration trying, Outcome metric, Rate, Journal. Each row links to library detail page. Mobile-responsive.

5. How RRM Compares to IVF

Supporting section, not the headline. Frame per VOC: "A different approach to a different question."

  • Live birth rates (RRM 32-66% cumulative vs IVF ~28% per cycle)
  • Multiple pregnancy (RRM: near zero vs IVF: 20-30% twin rate)
  • Preterm birth risk (IVF 4.24x, IUI 3.17x vs RRM baseline)
  • Cost (RRM fraction of IVF's $40K-$60K)
  • What you learn (RRM gives a diagnosis; IVF bypasses without one)

6. About the STORRM Registry

Brief section. Ongoing international registry at University of Utah. Future data source.

7. FAQ Section (FAQPage schema)

5-7 questions from persona objections and search queries:

  • "What are the success rates of RRM treatment?"
  • "Does RRM work after failed IVF?"
  • "How long does RRM treatment take to achieve pregnancy?"
  • "Is RRM effective for women over 35?"
  • "How do RRM success rates compare to IVF?"

  • "Is the evidence for RRM peer-reviewed?"

What This Page Is NOT

  • Not an attack on IVF. Per VOC: many patients carry grief about failed IVF. Frame comparison as information, not judgment.
  • Not a comprehensive RRM explainer. That's /what-is-rrm/.
  • Not a research library. That's /library/. This page synthesizes; the library stores.
  • Not a sales page. Per VOC: buyers are already motivated. Give them information, not persuasion.

Notes

  • The medRxiv preprint (James 2021) is not yet peer-reviewed. Flag on page with a note.
  • The Andrology study (Grande 2025) uses "natural fertility" not "RRM" but describes the same multidisciplinary, root-cause, couple-centered approach with fertility awareness integration. Classify as RRM-aligned.
