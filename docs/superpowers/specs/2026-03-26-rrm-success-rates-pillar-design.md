# RRM Success Rates Pillar Page -- Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**URL:** `/rrm-success-rates/`
**Author:** Dr. Naomi Whittaker, MD, Board-Certified OBGYN, MIGS, NFPMC, FCI
**Type:** Pillar guide (root-level, same pattern as `/what-is-rrm/`)

## Purpose

A single authoritative page synthesizing all published RRM outcomes data. Replaces the need to visit IIRRM's flat bullet list. Answers the question patients actually ask: "Does this work?"

## Audience

**Primary:** The Exhausted Patient (30-42, failed conventional treatment, trust damaged, needs evidence before committing) and The Values-Driven Couple (26-40, decided against IVF, needs confirmation evidence supports their choice).

**Secondary:** The Curious Generalist (OBGYN/family medicine, wants peer-reviewed evidence to evaluate) and The Frustrated OBGYN (wants outcome data to cite when colleagues question RRM).

**VOC constraints:**
- Empathy before proof. Recognition before statistics.
- OB/GYN comfort score is 1.1/5. These patients have earned their skepticism.
- Never position against IVF combatively. Frame as "a different approach to a different question."
- Buyers are already motivated. They lack information, not motivation.

## Page Structure

### 1. Recognition Section (above the fold)
Short, empathetic opening that validates the reader's experience before any data. Per VOC: "What I was told would work, didn't" is the shared origin story.

- H1: "RRM Success Rates: What the Research Shows"
- 1-2 sentences acknowledging the search ("You've been looking for answers. Maybe you've been told IVF is your only option. Maybe you've already tried it.")
- Bridge to evidence ("Peer-reviewed research from 8 countries tells a different story.")

No stats in this section. Recognition first.

### 2. Key Takeaways Box
Styled callout (same pattern as `/what-is-rrm/`). 4-5 bold lead statements with "RRM" as subject (AEO-optimized, complete citable statements):

- **RRM research reports live birth rates of 32% to 66% depending on patient population and follow-up duration.** The largest cohort (1,310 couples) reported an adjusted cumulative take-home baby rate of 62.1%.
- **RRM has been studied in over 4,500 couples across 8 countries on 4 continents.** Published data spans the United States, Canada, Ireland, Spain, Italy, Germany, Australia, and Ukraine.
- **RRM achieves pregnancies even after failed IVF.** In one study of 403 couples where 100% had prior IVF failure, the adjusted cumulative live birth rate was 32.1%, with 92% of babies born at full term.
- **RRM reduces "unexplained" infertility to under 10% of cases.** One multicenter study found that rigorous diagnostic evaluation reduced idiopathic infertility from the typical 30-50% to just 8%.
- **RRM births are overwhelmingly singleton and full-term.** Multiple pregnancy rates are near zero, compared to 20-30% twin rates with IVF.

### 3. The Evidence: RRM Outcomes by Study
Narrative synthesis organized by theme, not chronologically. Each subsection weaves 2-3 studies together with a clear patient-facing takeaway. Every statistic has a superscript citation linking to the library detail page.

**Section 3a: "Does RRM work for infertility?"**
Core outcomes narrative. Anchored by Sanchez-Mendez (1,310 couples, 62.1%), Stanford/Boyle JABFM (1,072 couples, 52.8%), and Tham Canada (108 couples, 66%). Emphasize: these are real patients in real clinics, not controlled trial participants. Multiple countries, multiple clinical settings, consistent results.

**Section 3b: "What if I've already tried IVF?"**
Anchored by de Groot/Boyle 2018 (403 couples, 100% prior IVF failure, 32.1% live birth). The Exhausted Patient's section. Frame: "RRM often uncovers treatable conditions that were never diagnosed during the IVF process." Reference the Andrology study (36% pregnancy rate in prior-ART-failure couples).

**Section 3c: "Does age matter?"**
Honest age-stratified data. Pull from Sanchez-Mendez (age-stratified rates in full text), Andrology Italy (~50% pregnancy rate in women <34, ~30% in <40). Acknowledge the real constraint without dismissing the concern. Counter the "clock anxiety" pressure per VOC.

**Section 3d: "What about male factor infertility?"**
Anchored by Grande/Andrology 2025. 87% of male partners had identifiable, treatable causes. Idiopathic infertility reduced from 30-50% to 8%. 40.9% spontaneous pregnancy. Frame: "The man matters. RRM evaluates both partners."

**Section 3e: "How long does it take?"**
Time-to-conception data. Boyle JRRM: average 12 months to conception for live birth patients. Marshell: 77% of pregnancies within 6 months, 92.5% within 12 months. Compare honestly to IVF timelines (2-3 cycles over 9-18 months for most couples).

### 4. Study Comparison Table
Interactive/sortable table for the detail-oriented reader (clinicians, researchers, The Researcher psychographic). All 10 outcomes studies with columns:

| Study | Year | Country | N | Mean age | Prior ART % | Duration trying | Outcome metric | Rate | Journal |

Each row links to the library detail page. Mobile-responsive (card layout on small screens).

### 5. How RRM Compares to IVF
Supporting section, not the headline. Uses the 2 ART risk studies plus IVF per-cycle stats from published literature already cited in `/what-is-rrm/`.

Frame per VOC: "A different approach to a different question." Not "RRM is better than IVF" but "Here is what each approach offers, costs, and risks."

Key comparisons:
- Live birth rates (RRM 32-66% cumulative vs IVF ~28% per cycle, ~50% cumulative over 3 cycles)
- Multiple pregnancy (RRM: near zero vs IVF: 20-30% twin rate)
- Preterm birth risk (IVF 4.24x, IUI 3.17x vs RRM baseline)
- Cost (RRM fraction of IVF's $40K-$60K average)
- What you learn (RRM gives a diagnosis; IVF bypasses without one)

### 6. About the STORRM Registry
Brief section explaining the ongoing international registry at University of Utah. Future data source. Links to IIRRM STORRM page.
