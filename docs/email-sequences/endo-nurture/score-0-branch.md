## Email 2 (Day 5): "Symptoms women dismiss as normal"

**Subject Line Options:**
A. The symptoms most women never mention to their doctor
B. "I just thought that was normal"

---

There is a short list of symptoms that women rarely report because they have been told, directly or indirectly, that they are normal.

They are not normal. They are worth investigating.

The five most commonly dismissed:

**Painful periods.** Not mild discomfort. Curled up, heating pad on, missing a day of work. "Everyone has cramps" is not a diagnosis.

**Tail-end brown bleeding.** That dark spotting at the end of a period is not leftover tissue clearing out. It can be a sign that the uterus is not contracting efficiently. Many RRM clinicians track this as a clinical marker.

**Pain during sex.** Dismissed for years. Often attributed to stress, positioning, or anxiety. It has a physiological cause that is findable with the right workup.

**Severe bloating before menstruation.** Not general bloating. Significant, cyclical distension that follows the pattern of the cycle.

**Period pain that requires more than standard pain relief.** Needing a fetal position or prescription medication to get through the first two days is not a baseline to accept.

If any of these sound familiar, they are not something you have to live with. You can retake the survey, or forward this to someone who might recognize herself in this list.

## Email 3 (Day 10): "One in ten"

**Subject Line Options:**
A. One in ten
B. If someone came to mind

---

One in ten women has endometriosis. Most of them do not know it yet.

The median time from first symptom to diagnosis is nine years. Nine years of symptoms that were documented, questioned, and then explained away. A lot of time to lose.

If someone came to mind while you were reading these emails, send them the survey link. It is free. It takes five minutes. And it may be the first time someone takes her symptoms seriously.
