## Body

You have had a week with your results.

If you have an appointment coming up, here is what to bring.

**Five questions worth asking:**

1. Has a diagnostic laparoscopy been discussed as part of my workup, or are we treating symptoms without confirming a diagnosis?

2. If endometriosis is found during surgery, what is your approach: excision or ablation? (These are not the same procedure. The evidence strongly favors excision.)

3. Do you use adhesion prevention barriers during surgery?

4. Have my hormone levels been measured with timed bloodwork across my cycle, or only a single random draw?

5. If you are recommending suppressive medications, what is the plan to investigate the underlying condition while I am on them?

These are not hostile questions. They are the questions a well-informed patient asks. The doctor's answers will tell you a lot.

---

If your doctor suggests suppressive medications as the first step, that is not automatically wrong. But it is worth asking: what are we investigating underneath? Suppression without investigation is not a treatment plan. It is a pause button. Disease can progress on that pause.
