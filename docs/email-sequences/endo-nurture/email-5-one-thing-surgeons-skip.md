## Body

Hi [First Name],

Most women preparing for endo surgery have never heard of adhesion prevention. Most surgeons do not prioritize it.

Here is what that means for your outcome.

After excision, your body heals. But healing means scar tissue. If a surgeon removes endometriosis without addressing the environment left behind, adhesions can form where the disease was. Tissue binds to tissue. Organs shift. Pain continues, or returns, under a different name.

Half the outcome is the excision. The other half is what the surgeon does to prevent adhesions from replacing the disease.

Proper technique, barrier agents, and careful tissue handling are not extras. They are the second half of the operation. A surgeon who treats adhesion prevention as an afterthought is, in effect, finishing only half the job.

Before any surgical consultation, ask this: "What adhesion prevention measures do you use?"

The answer will tell you whether your surgeon treats this as a routine step or an afterthought.
