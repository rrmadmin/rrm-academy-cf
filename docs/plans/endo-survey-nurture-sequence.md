# Endo Survey Post-Survey Nurture Sequence

## Status: Planning (2026-03-17)

## Key Insight

Survey takers and Masterclass buyers are at different journey points separated by **months**, not days.

- **Survey taker:** "I think something is wrong" -- pre-diagnosis, often pre-appointment
- **Masterclass buyer:** "I have surgery scheduled or plan to schedule" -- surgical decision already made

The email sequence is NOT a funnel to Masterclass. It is a trust-building relationship that sells the Prep Kit (near-term need) and keeps Naomi top-of-mind for when the Masterclass becomes relevant months later.

## Existing Survey Copy (emails must continue, not repeat)

The survey results page already delivers these emotional beats:

- **High Tier 1 (5+):** "You are not crazy." + full validation + "you deserve answers"
- **Mid range:** "Your symptoms tell a story worth investigating."
- **Low:** "Worth keeping on your radar." + "Trust that instinct."

PDF Page 2 reinforces with 9-year delay stat and Naomi's personal framing. Emails pick up WHERE THE SURVEY LEFT OFF -- "now what?" not "you matter."

## Sequence Architecture

### Main Sequence (Score > 0)

**Email 1 (Day 0): "You have your results. Here is what to do next."**
- PDF re-delivery (in case they lost the download)
- NOT validation (they already got it). Three concrete next steps before their next appointment
- Micro-action: "Save the PDF to your phone so it is there when you need it"
- One-line Naomi credibility (one sentence, not a paragraph)
- P.S. for providers: "If you took this for a patient, reply and let me know"
- Footer disclaimer on every email

**Email 2 (Day 3): "What your doctor should be looking for"**
- Bridge from "your symptoms tell a story" (survey) to "here is how a clinician reads it"
- One concrete clinical contrast: RRM workup vs. standard "go on birth control"
- Seed surgeon selection: "Understanding your pattern is step one. Knowing what kind of clinician to look for is step two."
- Social proof: survey count ("X women have used this tool")
- Micro-action: "Write down the top 3 symptoms from your PDF that concern you most"

**Email 3 (Day 7): "The questions your doctor won't expect you to ask"**
- Framed as ammunition, not education
- 3-5 specific questions to ask at their next appointment (free value, not gated)
- "If your doctor suggests birth control as the first and only step, here is what to ask instead"
- Natural Prep Kit intro: "I turned these questions and more into a printable checklist. I made it because I have seen doctors dismiss women who come in unprepared." (clinical tool framing, not product)
- $12, positioned as "I deliberately priced this to be accessible"
- Buyer language: "the right questions," "tools needed to pick the best doctor for me"

**Email 4 (Day 12): "Those ten years are not neutral time"**
- Adapted from "Ten Years Is Too Long" blog post
- Suppression masks symptoms, disease progresses -- framed through patient experience, not pharmacology
- Real cost of delayed diagnosis (fertility window, progression, repeated failed treatments)
- Link to 2-3 library articles
- Prep Kit reminder for non-buyers

**Email 5 (Day 18, high scorers only): "One thing most surgeons skip"**
- Adhesion prevention as the surprise (1.2/5 awareness -- genuinely new info for them)
- "Most surgeons don't do this. Here is how you find out if yours does."
- Masterclass as AWARENESS only: "When you reach the point of evaluating surgeons, this exists." Not a pitch
- One testimonial quote

**Email 6 (Day 24, high scorers only): "You do not have to figure this out alone"**
- Mirrors the survey results CTA headline
- Naomi's own story (earned after 24 days)
- Masterclass awareness: "the course I built because it didn't exist when I needed it"
- Find a Provider link (when live)
- Provider acknowledgment: "If you counsel women with endo, this is the surgical context training never gave you"
- Transition: "I will keep sharing research and resources. Stay in touch."

### Score 0 Branch (3 emails)

**Email 1 (Day 0):** "Your score was low. Here is what that means and what to watch for."
**Email 2 (Day 5):** "Symptoms women dismiss as normal" (addresses underreporting/normalization)
**Email 3 (Day 10):** "Know someone who might need this?" (share/referral CTA)

### Segmentation

- **Total score** drives sequence length (0 / low / medium / high)
- **Tier 1 count** drives content personalization within emails
- **Behavioral layer** (future): opened/clicked signals for acceleration or re-engagement

### Exit Conditions

- **Bought Prep Kit:** Enter 2-email bridge (usage tips, awareness of Masterclass for later)
- **Completed sequence without purchase:** Roll into newsletter/commentary cadence
- **Masterclass conversion:** Happens organically months later, driven by life events not email cadence
- **Unsubscribe/bounce:** Track which email number spikes occur -- content-market-fit signal
- **Behavioral re-entry:** If non-converter later clicks commentary or library content, condensed 2-email Prep Kit reminder

## Prep Kit Product: "Endo Appointment Prep Kit" ($12)

- Questions to bring to your next OB/GYN visit (organized by stage: undiagnosed, diagnosed, pre-surgical, post-surgical)
- 1-page symptom log template (structured around the same tiers from the survey)
- Red flags checklist: signs you are being dismissed, signs you have a good doctor
- "What your doctor should be doing and isn't" 1-page reference
- Branded PDF, designed (not a text dump)
- Framed as clinical tool Naomi built, not a product she sells

## Three-Agent Critique Summary

### VOC Findings
- #1 buyer motivation (surgeon selection) must appear by Email 2, not Email 4
- "Motivated but under-informed" -- don't validate what they already believe, equip them
- "You're not crazy" is already delivered by the survey -- don't repeat
- Provider audience (20% of Masterclass buyers) needs acknowledgment, not a separate track
- Key buyer phrases to use: "the right questions," "I don't know what I don't know," "answers you won't find in a search"

### Gianna Findings
- Prep Kit must be framed as clinical tool, not product. "I made this because doctors dismiss women who come in unprepared"
- Every clinical email needs footer disclaimer (education, not medical advice)
- Don't imply survey score is determinative -- "patterns clinicians recognize" not "you likely have endo"
- Couple-centered evaluation (male factor) should appear in fertility-relevant emails only
- Naomi would never soften with "journey" or "process" -- clinical directness is reassuring

### CXL Findings
- Optimal tripwire window: Day 5-8 after lead magnet delivery
- Micro-commitment CTAs in every email (save, print, write down, reply)
- No artificial scarcity or urgency -- real medical cost of delay is the urgency lever
- Score 0 segment has referral potential -- don't discard
- Confidence transfer, not information delivery, is the conversion mechanism
- Post-sequence long-tail nurture is essential (average health-education purchase cycle: 30-90 days)
- Find a Provider is both value-add and behavioral intent signal
