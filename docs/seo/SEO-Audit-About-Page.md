# SEO Audit: RRM Academy About Page

**Date:** 2026-02-19
**Page:** rrmacademy.org/about
**Overall Assessment:** The page is written for believers, not searchers. Most organic traffic comes from people who don't know what RRM is yet. The page needs to serve the uninitiated searcher first (define, prove, link) then inspire.

---

## 1. Title Tag & Meta Description

**Current title:** `About | RRM Academy`

**Problem:** "About" is a zero-value keyword. No primary keyword in the title. "Restorative Reproductive Medicine" does not appear.

**Recommended title (under 60 chars):**
```
About RRM Academy | Restorative Reproductive Medicine Education
```

**Recommended meta description (under 160 chars):**
```
RRM Academy is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit offering clinician-led courses in restorative reproductive medicine. Founded by Dr. Naomi Whittaker, board-certified OBGYN.
```

---

## 2. Heading Hierarchy

**Current structure:**
- No H1
- H2: "A Different Approach"
- H2: "How We Help"
- H2: "Meet Our Founder"
- H2: "Join Our Mission"

**Problems:**
- Missing H1. The single most important on-page heading for SEO.
- H2s are vague. "A Different Approach" and "How We Help" contain zero keywords.
- No H3s. Bullet points under "How We Help" are bold text, not semantic subheadings.

**Recommended structure:**
```
H1: About RRM Academy: Education in Restorative Reproductive Medicine

H2: What Is Restorative Reproductive Medicine?
   (replaces "A Different Approach")

H2: How RRM Academy Helps Patients and Professionals
   (replaces "How We Help")
   H3: Clinician-Led Online Courses
   H3: Patient Education and Empowerment
   H3: Professional Training and CME Resources
   H3: Evidence-Based Research Library

H2: Meet Our Founder: Dr. Naomi Whittaker, MD, OBGYN, MIGS
   (adds her name and credentials, which are searched)

H2: Support Our Nonprofit Mission
   (replaces "Join Our Mission", adds "nonprofit" keyword)
```

| PCOS natural treatment | 5,000-10,000 | High | Not present |

### Critical keyword gaps

1. **"NaProTECHNOLOGY"** appears only once, buried in founder bio. 1,000-2,000 monthly searches. Needs to appear in the "What is RRM" section.

2. **Condition-specific keywords are completely absent.** The page never mentions endometriosis, PCOS, infertility, recurrent miscarriage, hormonal imbalance. These are the terms patients search for. The page speaks in abstractions ("health issues," "symptoms," "problems").

3. **"Alternative to IVF"** mentioned once dismissively. Not positioned as the searched-for alternative.

4. **"Nonprofit" / "501(c)(3)"** mentioned once in passing. Donors need this more prominent.

### Add this paragraph after the RRM description:

```
Conditions commonly addressed through RRM include endometriosis, polycystic ovary
syndrome (PCOS), unexplained infertility, recurrent pregnancy loss, irregular or
painful menstrual cycles, hormonal imbalances, ovulatory dysfunction, and male
factor infertility. For many patients, RRM offers an effective alternative to IVF
and other assisted reproductive technologies by treating the underlying cause
rather than bypassing it.
```

This single addition targets 6+ high-value search terms.

## 4. Content Gaps

### A. Conditions treated (competitors all list these explicitly)
Endometriosis, PCOS, unexplained infertility, recurrent miscarriage, irregular/painful periods, hormonal imbalances, male factor infertility, PMS, ovulation disorders, thyroid-related fertility issues. Your page names zero conditions.

### B. Statistics and proof points
The page says "3,100+ research publications" nowhere. It says "12 courses" nowhere. Add:
- "12 expert-led online courses"
- "3,100+ curated research publications in our RRM Research Library"
- Any course completion numbers, satisfaction rates, or outcome data

### C. "Who is this for?" section
The page never explicitly states its two audiences in a scannable way:
```
For Patients and Couples: If you're dealing with endometriosis, PCOS, infertility,
or unexplained symptoms and want to understand your options beyond birth control and IVF.

For Healthcare Professionals: If you're a physician, nurse practitioner, or allied
health provider looking to integrate restorative approaches into your practice.
```

### D. FAQ section
These questions are frequently searched and should be answered on or linked from this page:
- "What is restorative reproductive medicine?"
- "How is RRM different from IVF?"
- "What conditions does RRM treat?"
- "Is NaProTechnology evidence-based?"
- "How do I find an RRM doctor?"

### E. Founder credentials are undersold
Current bio omits MIGS designation, training institutions, and Pope Paul VI Institute affiliation. Every competing provider site (Healthgrades, FACTS About Fertility, LinkedIn) lists more about Dr. Whittaker than her own organization's About page does.

## 6. E-E-A-T Signals

This is a **YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) page**. Google applies the highest E-E-A-T scrutiny.

### Experience: WEAK
- No patient testimonials or outcome stories
- No "X years of clinical experience" or case numbers
- No mention of where Dr. Whittaker practices
- Fix: Add "With over [X] years of clinical experience..." to founder bio. Link to patient stories on the blog.

### Expertise: MODERATE but undersold
- Dr. Whittaker's credentials are severely truncated
- No mention of publications, research contributions, or speaking engagements
- No mention of other medical advisors or course instructors
- Fix: Expand founder bio. Add "Our Medical Team" or "Course Faculty" if other clinicians contribute.

### Authoritativeness: WEAK
- No affiliations with recognized medical institutions mentioned
- No mention of IIRRM, Pope Paul VI Institute, Creighton University, or ACOG
- The RRM Research Library (3,100+ publications) is an enormous authority signal completely absent from the page
- Fix: Add "Our Research Library contains over 3,100 peer-reviewed publications on restorative reproductive medicine, one of the largest curated collections in the field."

### Trustworthiness: MODERATE
- 501(c)(3) status mentioned (good)
- EIN not on the page
- No physical address or location hint
- No "how we're funded" transparency
- Fix: Add EIN and funding transparency statement: "As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit (EIN: 93-4594315), RRM Academy is funded through course enrollment, donations, and the Save the Uterus Club membership program."

## 9. Specific Content Rewrites

### Rewrite 1: Opening paragraph

**Current:**
> At RRM Academy, we believe women deserve more than temporary fixes and one-size-fits-all solutions. Our mission is to provide expert-led, evidence-based education that empowers both patients and healthcare professionals to pursue a new model of care, one that seeks to find and treat the root cause of health issues.

**Recommended:**
> RRM Academy provides expert-led, evidence-based education in Restorative Reproductive Medicine (RRM) for patients and healthcare professionals. Through 12 online courses and a research library of 3,100+ publications, we equip individuals and clinicians with the knowledge to find and treat the root causes of conditions like endometriosis, PCOS, infertility, and recurrent miscarriage, rather than masking symptoms or bypassing the body's natural function.

**Why:** The original opens with a belief statement. Google does not index beliefs. The rewrite leads with what the organization IS and DOES, includes the full term with acronym parenthetical, names specific conditions, and front-loads proof points.

### Rewrite 2: Section heading + first paragraph

**Current heading:** "A Different Approach"
**Recommended heading:** "What Is Restorative Reproductive Medicine?"

**Current first paragraph:**
> For too long, women's health has been dominated by approaches that mask symptoms with hormonal medications or bypass problems with procedures like IVF. This leaves many feeling dismissed and without real answers.

**Recommended:**
> Restorative Reproductive Medicine (RRM) is an approach within conventional medicine that works cooperatively with the body to diagnose and treat the underlying causes of reproductive health conditions. Unlike approaches that mask symptoms with hormonal medications or bypass natural function with assisted reproductive technologies like IVF, RRM uses standard and advanced diagnostic tools to investigate the root cause, whether hormonal, anatomical, or inflammatory, and restore natural function.

**Why:** Current version defines RRM only by what it is NOT. The rewrite defines it by what it IS first. Matches "what is RRM" search intent.

### Rewrite 3: Final CTA paragraph

**Current:**
> We envision a future where every woman is heard, valued, and empowered on her healthcare journey...

**Recommended:**
> Whether you are a patient seeking answers about endometriosis, infertility, or hormonal health, a healthcare professional expanding your clinical skills, or a supporter who shares our vision, there are many ways to get involved:
>
> - **Explore our courses** (link to /courses): 12 clinician-led online courses on topics from endometriosis surgery to understanding your fertility cycle
> - **Browse the RRM Research Library** (link to /library): 3,100+ curated research publications, freely accessible
> - **Join the Save the Uterus Club** (link to /save-the-uterus-club): support our 501(c)(3) nonprofit mission through monthly giving
>
> RRM Academy is a project of the Restorative Reproductive Medicine Foundation, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (EIN: 93-4594315).
