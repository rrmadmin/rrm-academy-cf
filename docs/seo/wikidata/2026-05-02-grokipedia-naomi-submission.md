## Article body draft

Naomi M. Whittaker (born Naomi Sasin) is an American board-certified obstetrician-gynecologist and fellowship-trained surgeon practicing in central Pennsylvania, and the founder of RRM Academy, an online education platform for restorative reproductive medicine [3][4][6]. She holds the Focused Practice Designation in Minimally Invasive Gynecologic Surgery from the American Board of Obstetrics and Gynecology and is a Certified FertilityCare Medical Consultant in the Creighton Model FertilityCare System [3][4][13].

### Early life and family background

Whittaker is the daughter of Polish political refugees who fled Communist Poland in 1984 under the cover of a stated vacation to East Germany, before the Berlin Wall came down [8][9]. After the family was accepted into the United States and matched with a sponsor, Whittaker was born in the United States in 1986, making her a first-generation Polish American [8]. She has cited her family physician as the figure who drew her to medicine in childhood [8].

### Education

Whittaker attended Cathedral High School in Indianapolis (2001-2005) and completed undergraduate studies at Purdue University (2006-2009), where she earned a Bachelor of Science in Neurobiology and Physiology with a minor in Psychology [14]. As an undergraduate she contributed to a peer-reviewed bioavailability study of grape-seed polyphenols published in the Journal of Alzheimer's Disease in 2009 [15].

### Medical training and fellowship

She earned her Doctor of Medicine degree from Creighton University School of Medicine in Omaha, Nebraska, in 2013 [3][4][14]. She completed a four-year residency in obstetrics and gynecology at the University of Illinois College of Medicine in Peoria from 2013 to 2017, training at OSF Saint Francis Medical Center and affiliated hospitals [4][14]. From August 2017 to July 2018 she served as a St. John Paul II Research Fellow in Medical and Surgical NaProTechnology at the Pope Paul VI Institute for the Study of Human Reproduction in Omaha [10][16].

### Board certification and licensure

Whittaker has been certified by the American Board of Obstetrics and Gynecology since February 2020 [14][30]. She also holds the ABOG Focused Practice Designation in Minimally Invasive Gynecologic Surgery (MIGS) [3][4]. She has been licensed to practice medicine in Pennsylvania since August 2018 [14] and has held prior licensure in Nebraska, Illinois, and Virginia [4]. Her National Provider Identifier is 1881034908 [17].

### Clinical practice

Whittaker is Physician Lead at UPMC Divine Mercy Women's Health, a women's health practice within the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center's Central Pennsylvania network, headquartered at 225 Grandview Avenue, Suite 302, Camp Hill, Pennsylvania [3][6][18]. She has held the role since December 2021, having previously served as an OBGYN physician at Holy Spirit Hospital in Camp Hill from 2018 to 2021 under successive Geisinger and Penn State Health management, with a concurrent faculty appointment in student education at Geisinger College of Medicine and Penn State Health [14]. She holds hospital privileges at UPMC Harrisburg, Penn State Health Holy Spirit Medical Center, and Geisinger Medical Center [3][4][19].

### Surgical specialties

Whittaker's surgical practice centers on minimally invasive gynecologic surgery and fertility-restoring procedures [3][4]. Reported areas of focus include excision of endometriosis with adhesion-prevention technique, microsurgical tubal anastomosis for tubal blockage and prior tubal ligation, ovarian wedge resection for polycystic ovary syndrome, uterine septum repair, and exploratory laparoscopy for occult or "silent" endometriosis [3][20]. In 2024 she presented a method she terms S-MAP (Systematic Mapping of the Abdomen and Pelvis) at the American Association of Gynecologic Laparoscopists 53rd Global Congress on MIGS, formalizing a diagnostic-laparoscopy mapping approach attributed to Thomas W. Hilgers [21][28]. A surgical case report of a Stage IV endometriosis patient who subsequently achieved spontaneous pregnancy was published by the International Institute for Restorative Reproductive Medicine of America [22].

### Restorative reproductive medicine and NaProTechnology

Whittaker practices within the discipline of restorative reproductive medicine, an approach that diagnoses and treats the underlying causes of reproductive dysfunction rather than bypassing them [10][23]. She integrates Creighton Model FertilityCare cycle charting and biomarker analysis with surgical and medical management of endometriosis, polycystic ovary syndrome, recurrent pregnancy loss, premature ovarian insufficiency, and luteal phase deficiency [10][24]. Independent profiles have described her as one of a small number of United States obstetrician-gynecologists with formal fellowship training in NaProTechnology [10][8].

### RRM Academy and educational work

In 2022 Whittaker founded RRM Academy (rrmacademy.org), an online educational platform for patients and healthcare professionals operated by the Restorative Reproductive Medicine Foundation, a 501(c)(3) public charity (EIN 93-4594315) [6][7][25]. The platform offers professional and patient courses on infertility, endometriosis surgery, postpartum mental health, and premature ovarian insufficiency, and maintains a curated research library [6][24]. Whittaker also serves as an instructor for the FACTS (Fertility Appreciation Collaborative to Teach the Science) elective, a fertility-awareness curriculum for medical students and clinicians [10][26]. She presented at the 2025 FACTS Conference in Pittsburgh on premature ovarian insufficiency and on restorative surgery for fertility enhancement [10].

### Publications and presentations

Whittaker maintains an active research record under ORCID identifier 0000-0003-3706-3112, with 17 indexed publications and conference presentations [5]. Her undergraduate co-authorship in the Journal of Alzheimer's Disease (2009) examined the bioavailability of grape-seed-extract catechins in a rat model relevant to neurodegenerative disease [15]. During medical school at Creighton she co-authored multiple oncology abstracts published in the Journal of Clinical Oncology and presented at the American Society of Clinical Oncology Quality Care Symposium and other meetings, drawing on the National Cancer Database [27]. During residency she presented case reports and ethical analyses at American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists national and district meetings, including a case of diamine oxidase deficiency proposed as a mechanism for postpartum anaphylaxis (ACOG 2017) and serial case reports on bilateral renal agenesis and serial amnioinfusion (ACOG 2015 and 2016) [5]. Her commentary on the IVF industry, titled "The Hidden Costs of IVF: Heartbreak, Health Risks, and Helplessness," was published in MercatorNet in February 2018 [9]. In 2026 she co-authored the chapter "Tuboplasty" (with William Nolan, first author) in the CRC Press Textbook of Minimally Invasive Gynecologic Surgery, covering microsurgical tubal repair techniques [29].

### Awards and recognition

Whittaker received the Honorary Award of the American Academy of FertilityCare Professionals in 2024 [1]. The AAFCP Honorary Award is a juried recognition that has been conferred 25 times across the 1985-2024 span; named co-recipients in the same award roll include Thomas W. Hilgers (1985), John J. and Evelyn Billings (1991), Mother Teresa of Calcutta (1993), Cardinal William J. Levada (2011), Joseph Stanford (2015), Janet Smith (2021), Sr. Renee Mirkes (2022), and Tiffany Hoeckelman (2023) [1]. Whittaker was the subject of a March 2026 profile in the Ethics and Public Policy Center "Technically Human" series, an independent third-party feature on restorative reproductive medicine and the purpose of medicine [2].

### Affiliations

Whittaker is a member of the American Association of Gynecologic Laparoscopists [21], a Certified FertilityCare Medical Consultant through FertilityCare Centers of America [13], a member of the International Institute for Restorative Reproductive Medicine [22], and an instructor with FACTS About Fertility [26]. She is the founder of RRM Academy and is associated with the Restorative Reproductive Medicine Foundation, the parent 501(c)(3) charity [6][7].

### Personal life

Whittaker is married and has six children. Her oldest child was born during her medical school years.
