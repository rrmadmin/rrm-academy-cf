Version A = current. Version B = summary block + answer capsules under each H2.

A: Current (No Capsules)

NaProTechnology Surgery: A Restorative Approach to Fertility and Gynecologic Health

Naomi Whittaker, MD

NaProTechnology (Natural Procreative Technology) Surgery is a specialized form of gynecologic surgery focused on diagnosing and treating underlying reproductive disorders to restore normal anatomy and function of the uterus, fallopian tubes, and ovaries.

It is part of a broader NaProTechnology approach that works with a woman's natural menstrual cycle (using the Creighton Model fertility charting) rather than bypassing it.

In contrast to conventional infertility treatments (which often suppress or bypass natural fertility, as with IVF), NaProTechnology views problems like infertility as symptoms of underlying disease and seeks to correct those root causes so that couples can conceive naturally.

Surgical NaPro interventions are therefore aimed at repairing abnormalities (endometriosis, blocked tubes, ovarian cysts, uterine defects, etc.) in a way that optimizes fertility while improving overall gynecologic health.

Advantages Over Other Treatments

  1. Treating Root Causes vs. Bypassing: A key advantage of NaProTechnology is its commitment to identifying and fixing the underlying gynecologic issues causing infertility or pain, rather than bypassing them. Instead of using IVF to work around blocked tubes or endometriosis, NaPro surgeons will remove endometriosis or repair tubes so the couple can conceive through normal intercourse. This restorative approach not only helps achieve pregnancy but can also relieve symptoms, improve long-term reproductive health, and provide answers to patients.

  2. "Near Adhesion-Free" Surgical Technique: NaPro surgeons employ meticulous techniques and adhesion-prevention strategies to make procedures "near adhesion-free." Published results show adhesion scores dropping from 33.3 to 6.0 over a decade with extensive Gore-Tex barrier use.

  3. Fertility Outcomes Comparable or Superior to IVF: More than 60% of couples conceived within 24 months and roughly 70% by 36 months. These cumulative pregnancy rates are similar to or higher than the 45-55% cumulative live-birth rate achieved after multiple IVF cycles. Importantly, virtually all resulting pregnancies are singleton births, eliminating the risk of high-order multiples.

  4. Fewer Health Risks and Ethical Concerns: No ovarian hyperstimulation, egg retrieval surgery, or embryo manipulation. No leftover embryos to freeze or discard. NaPro care tends to be more affordable than IVF since it uses standard medical and surgical treatments often covered by insurance.

  5. Holistic Benefits: By fixing problems like endometriosis, polycystic ovaries, or fibroids, women often experience relief of chronic pain, improved menstrual regularity, and better gynecologic health -- benefits that chronic birth control use or IVF don't provide.

Specialized Surgical Techniques in NaProTechnology

NaPro surgeons are trained in advanced techniques that set their approach apart:

  • Meticulous Adhesion Prevention: Delicate tissue handling, microsurgical suturing with permanent non-reactive sutures (4-0 Prolene), imbricating stitch technique, and bioinert barriers (Gore-Tex membranes) placed between raw surfaces. These are removed in a second-look surgery after initial healing.

  • "Near-Contact" Laparoscopy: Moving the laparoscope extremely close to tissues to inspect for subtle lesions missed at standard distance. Very small endometriosis implants hidden in peritoneal folds become visible only when viewed at high magnification up close.

  • Laser and Microsurgery: CO2 laser for precise incisions (e.g., ovarian wedge resection for PCOS), laser vaporization for superficial endometriosis. Extremely precise cutting and coagulation with reduced tissue trauma.

  • Pelvic Excision and Reconstructive Surgery (PEARS): Aggressive excision of all visible endometriotic lesions combined with reconstructive repair of affected organs. Treats endometriosis like plastic reconstructive surgery of the pelvis, maximizing chances for pain relief and natural fertility.

  • Selective Hysterosalpingography (Tubal Recanalization): A fine catheter or guidewire is inserted through the cervix into a blocked fallopian tube to dislodge debris and reopen the tube. Can often restore tubal patency without full surgery.

  • Hysteroscopic and Reconstructive Procedures: Removing polyps, fibroids, or uterine septa. Tubal reanastomosis (reversing tubal ligation). Myomectomy with adhesion prevention. NaPro revives classic fertility surgical techniques with modern enhancements.

Conditions Commonly Treated with NaPro Surgery

NaPro surgical applications span a wide range of reproductive disorders:

  • Endometriosis: Surgically excised as completely as possible. NaPro surgeons remove even small implants rather than just cauterizing them. After excising lesions, they repair surfaces with fine sutures to prevent scar tissue. Surgical removal gives the best outcomes for pain relief and subsequent fertility.

  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): Ovarian wedge resection using laser and meticulous suturing. Modern technique achieves about a 70% pregnancy rate, over twice as effective as clomiphene citrate (roughly 30%) and higher than reported IVF success in PCOS (roughly 23%).

  • Blocked or Damaged Fallopian Tubes: Laparoscopic microsurgery to remove adhesions, reconstruct damaged segments, or reverse tubal ligation. Selective HSG tubal recanalization for mild blockage. The goal: at least one open, functional tube for natural conception.

  • Uterine Fibroids and Abnormalities: Myomectomy with solid repair and adhesion prevention. Hysteroscopic removal of polyps, fibroids, or uterine septa. Even cesarean scar defects (isthmoceles) are in the scope of NaPro repair.

  • Pelvic Adhesions: Specialized adhesiolysis combined with adhesion barrier membranes, hydroflotation solutions, and microsurgical techniques to prevent reformation. Freeing bound-down tubes and ovaries can restore the path to natural pregnancy.

Patient Outcomes and Success Rates Compared to Conventional Methods

Infertility Treatment Success: A large NaPro cohort (1,045 patients) achieved a cumulative live-birth rate of more than 60% by 24 months, rising to roughly 70% by 36 months. This compares to about 45-55% cumulative live-birth rate after multiple IVF cycles.

Monthly fecundability on NaPro treatment was around 3.1% vs. 13.3% with IVF. IVF may produce quicker results per cycle, but the overall per-woman pregnancy rate ended up higher with NaPro because couples continued trying without the attrition seen in ART programs.

Comparative Studies: An Irish study (Stanford et al. 2008) reported 52.8% live births within 2 years. A Canadian study (Tham et al. 2012) reported 66% live births within 2 years. A comparison group of untreated infertile women had only a roughly 20% live birth rate.

Specific Conditions:

  • Endometriosis-related infertility: Surgical treatment yielded a 55.3% per-woman pregnancy rate vs. only 9.9% with IVF in moderate-to-severe cases.
  • PCOS: Ovarian wedge resection achieves roughly 70% pregnancy success, outperforming medical ovulation induction and IVF.
  • Tubal repairs: Tubal reanastomosis often yields pregnancy rates of 50% or more within a year or two.

Maternal and Fetal Outcomes: All pregnancies in the Canadian study were singletons. Majority were at term with healthy birth weights. No risk of high-order multiples, ovarian hyperstimulation, or other ART-related complications.

Notable Studies and Expert Opinions Supporting NaPro Surgery

NaProTechnology's surgical approach is supported by over four decades of research. Dr. Thomas Hilgers documented extensive experience in his textbook and reported "Near Adhesion-Free Reconstructive Pelvic Surgery" after 23 years of applying these techniques.

The AMA Journal of Ethics (2013) profiled NaProTechnology as a viable option, emphasizing its adhesion prevention achievement and strong pregnancy success rates. A meta-analysis supporting surgery over IVF in endometriosis cases was cited.

The Pope Paul VI Institute reports up to 80% of couples treated for PCOS or endometriosis eventually achieve pregnancy using NaPro methods. The American Academy of FertilityCare Professionals has trained hundreds of physicians worldwide.

Multiple peer-reviewed studies validate the approach, and experts stress that NaPro's emphasis on normalizing the woman's cycle leads to better outcomes not just for pregnancy but also for reducing miscarriages and improving gynecologic conditions. As awareness grows, NaProTechnology is increasingly recognized as a valid and effective option for treating infertility and gynecologic disorders.
