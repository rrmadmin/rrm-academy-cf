The average time to an endometriosis diagnosis is 7 to 10 years. That number has not meaningfully improved in decades. Women describe the same pattern over and over: real symptoms, real suffering, and years of being told it is normal.

We built the [3-Tier Endometriosis Symptom Self-Survey](https://rrmacademy.org/endo-survey) to put a clinical framework directly in women's hands. It is free, it is private, and it produces a report you can hand to your doctor.

## Where This Came From

Dr. Naomi Whittaker developed the 3-tier symptom framework from patterns she sees in her surgical practice. Certain symptoms cluster together in women who are later confirmed to have endometriosis. Others show up frequently as co-conditions. Some are low-specificity risk factors that, by themselves, would not raise a flag, but combined with the rest of the picture, they reinforce clinical suspicion.

The problem is that this clinical reasoning lives inside the heads of specialists like Dr. Whittaker. Most women never get access to it until they are already sitting in a surgeon's office, often after years of being dismissed. We wanted to change that.

## How the Survey Works

You go to [rrmacademy.org/endo-survey](https://rrmacademy.org/endo-survey), enter your email, and receive a private link. We use your email only to deliver the link. No mailing list. No marketing. The link expires after 90 days and can only be used once.

The survey presents 36 symptoms organized into three tiers.

**Tier 1 (Very High Suspicion):** 15 symptoms, 3 points each. These are the patterns most strongly associated with confirmed endometriosis. Vomiting with menstruation. ER visits for menstrual pain. Deep dyspareunia. Severe cramping that puts you in the fetal position. Recurrent tail-end brown bleeding. OTC pain medications that do nothing. Birth control that fails to help.

**Tier 2 (High Suspicion):** 15 symptoms, 2 points each. Systemic symptoms and co-conditions that frequently accompany the disease. IBS. Interstitial cystitis. Migraines. Severe premenstrual bloating. Heavy bleeding with iron deficiency. Short luteal phase. Low-quality cervical mucus.

**Tier 3 (Suspicion):** 6 symptoms, 1 point each. Lower-specificity markers: fatigue, nickel sensitivity, easy bruising, family history, autoimmune history.

You check everything that applies. The tool calculates a weighted score out of 81 and shows a breakdown by tier.

## The Results

The score alone is useful, but the interpretation is what matters. Dr. Whittaker wrote personalized clinical guidance for each scoring range.

A high Tier 1 count gets a direct message: your symptoms fit a strong pattern. A moderate score across multiple tiers gets a different message: this is worth investigating. A low score is treated honestly, but with the reminder that even a few of these symptoms are worth discussing with a knowledgeable provider.

The interpretation validates what women are experiencing without overstepping into diagnosis. Endometriosis can only be confirmed through surgery. This survey does not replace that. What it does is give you language and data for the conversation with your doctor.

## The PDF Report

After calculating your score, you can download a PDF. This is the part I want every woman to actually use.

The report includes your total score, tier-by-tier breakdowns with visual progress bars, and every symptom you selected, listed by tier. Print it. Bring it to your next appointment. Hand it to your doctor and let the data speak.

## Why This Matters

Endometriosis affects an estimated 1 in 10 women of reproductive age. The diagnostic delay is measured in years, not months. The consequences of that delay are real: disease progression, chronic pain, fertility loss, and the psychological toll of being told your suffering is not real.

The healthcare system is not going to fix this fast enough. So we are building tools that put information directly in women's hands. The 3-Tier survey is one piece. The [RRM Research Library](https://rrmacademy.org/library) with 3,000+ indexed studies is another. The commentary you are reading now is another.
