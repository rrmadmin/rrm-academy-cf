## 1. Abstract / Lede

- One-paragraph statement of the problem + the protocol + headline result.
- Problem: no standard criteria exist for how to perform a diagnostic laparoscopy [AAGL slide: "Conclusion" + transcript [MC2]].
- Proposed solution: NCL + S-MAP as a paired, reproducible protocol.
- Headline outcome from AAGL case series: 4 rare cancers found and cured in 3 years by a single non-oncologic surgeon using this approach; 3 of 3 infertility patients in the series achieved pregnancy [AAGL].

## 2. Background and Rationale

### 2.1 The Problem with Unstandardized Diagnostic Laparoscopy
- No uniform criteria define what "diagnostic laparoscopy" is in gynecology [AAGL Conclusion slide].
- Basic-approach practice norms documented in masterclass Basic-vs-Advanced table [MC2]:
  - Quick "zoomed-out" look, minimal photography, minimal biopsies.
  - Skip/do-not-label upper abdomen and appendix.
  - Obvious lesions only; no near-contact examination.
- Clinical consequence: endometriosis and non-gynecologic pathology missed in women told "your laparoscopy was normal."

### 2.2 Evidence That Subtle Disease Is Missed
- **Hilgers Table 63-1** — Repeat Laparoscopies when Previous Laparoscopy Was Considered Normal (N=46) [FACTS-LG slide 17; `chapter-63-diagnostic-laparoscopy-near-contact-approach`]:
  - With endometriosis: 41 / 46 = **89.1%**
  - With adhesive disease: 2 / 46 = 4.4%
  - Normal pelvis: 3 / 46 = **6.5%**
  - Implication: 93.5% of pelves previously declared "normal" had pathology when reassessed with NCL.
- Endometriosis lesions as small as 2 mm are identifiable and excisable under NCL [fact-extracted-838].
- Endometriosis has diverse appearances — black plastic, halo, retraction pockets, hemosiderin (brown), white scarring, red/clear polyps, vesicles, Allen–Masters Windows (deep peritoneal tunnels). Anything abnormal warrants suspicion [MC2].
- Teaching visual to reuse: the paired "Can you spot the endometriosis?" NCL images (unannotated vs. annotated with subtle-lesion circles) from @napro_fertility_surgeon [FACTS-LG slides 15–16] — strong demonstration that far-view lap is insufficient.

### 2.3 Historical Lineage (Attribution)

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Near-Contact Laparoscopy (NCL) — Technique 1
- Operative definition: "In addition to standard laparoscopic views, it involves moving the laparoscope very close to the peritoneal surface" [AAGL slide 4].
- Purpose cues [AAGL slide 4 + MC2]:
  - Magnified view + more detailed visualization.
  - Improved detection of small or subtle endometriosis lesions ("occult microscopic lesions").
  - Enables thorough diagnostic laparoscopy and, therefore, complete excision.
- Field analogy (Whittaker teaching metaphor): standing in a field looks healthy until you bend down and see the Easter eggs hidden in the grass [MC2].

### 3.2 Systematic Mapping of the Abdomen and Pelvis (S-MAP) — Technique 2
- Operative definition: "a methodical, standardized protocol for comprehensive examination of all abdominal and pelvic structures during laparoscopy, ensuring that no area of disease is overlooked" [GLOSS term_s-map].
- Core properties [AAGL slide 5]:
  - **Systematic / reproducible** — all areas inspected in a consistent manner; aids staging and documentation; beneficial for research, education, training.
  - **Routine** — reduces chance of missing abnormalities.
  - **Thorough** — potential to improve detection and patient outcomes.
- Anatomic coverage [AAGL slide 5 + MC2]:
  - **Upper abdomen:** diaphragm, gallbladder, liver edge.
  - **Lower abdomen:** cecum, appendix, terminal ileum.
  - **Pelvis:** anterior cul-de-sac, posterior cul-de-sac, uterosacral ligaments, ovarian fossae, pelvic sidewalls, Allen–Masters windows.
- Directional pattern: a fixed start → loop → end path following the same route every case [AAGL slide 5 diagram; MC2: "start the top of the uterus and go around, lifting things around, seeing every part of the pelvis and abdomen"].

### 3.3 Relationship Between NCL and S-MAP
- They are **complementary, not interchangeable**.
- S-MAP = *where* to look (coverage sequence); NCL = *how closely* to look at each station.
- Full protocol = "Do NCL at every station of S-MAP, every case." [synthesis of AAGL + MC2]

## 4. Protocol (Draft Methods Section)

*Outline only — exact station count + order will be set by Naomi. Suggested structure so she can fill in.*

### 4.1 Setup and Instruments
- Standard gyn laparoscopy setup; high-definition 0° and 30° scopes.
- Video documentation expected at every station (advanced approach column of Basic-vs-Advanced table) [MC2].
- Uterine manipulator, atraumatic graspers, tissue-sparing energy (e.g., laser for excision, per Case D) [AAGL Case D].
- Adhesion prevention barriers available from first case (Gore-Tex, etc.) [AAGL Case D; fact-extracted-796; MC2].

### 4.2 Standardized Station Sequence (fill-in)
*Proposed numbered sequence mirroring Whittaker's "same map every time" [MC2] + Hilgers Ch. 63 sequence:*
1. Umbilical entry verification — far-view orientation.
2. Upper abdomen survey: right hemi-diaphragm → falciform → left hemi-diaphragm → gallbladder → liver edge.
3. Descending path: right paracolic gutter → cecum → **appendix (inspection with appendectomy threshold criteria)**.
4. Transverse colon / omentum, terminal ileum.
5. Pelvic entry: anterior cul-de-sac → bladder peritoneum → round ligaments → broad ligaments.
6. Ovarian fossae (bilateral): ovary mobilization + NCL of fossa floor.
7. Uterosacral ligaments, posterior cul-de-sac, rectovaginal septum.
8. Sigmoid/rectal serosa, pararectal spaces.
9. NCL re-sweep: second pass at magnification on any suspicious surface.
10. Documentation loop: still + video capture of each station before closure.

*Each station bullet will expand to include: "what normal looks like at NCL / red-flag findings / biopsy threshold / documentation requirement."*

### 4.3 Biopsy, Excision, and Sampling Rules
- Bias toward biopsy of any abnormal peritoneum [MC2 Basic-vs-Advanced table: "many biopsies"].
- Excise rather than ablate when restorative intent [MC2; consistent with RRM excision-only framing].
- Appendix inspection is mandatory; offer appendectomy if indicated [MC2; AAGL Cases C + D].
- Adhesion prevention (Gore-Tex or equivalent) integrated into the same case, not deferred [MC2; AAGL Case D; [FACTS-LG] Case LG surgery #3: "Gore-tex membrane implant for adhesion as well as Seprafilm/steroid/LR slurry"; Cochrane n=1,200 [fact-extracted-796]].
- Evidence base for integrated adhesion prevention — Hilgers *Near Adhesion-Free Reconstructive Pelvic Surgery: Three Distinct Phases of Progress Over 23 Years* (J Gynecol Surg, March 2010) [FACTS-LG slide 18]:
  - Mean Total AFS adhesion score (before → after): Phase I (1987–1993) 33.8 → 18.1; Phase II (1994–2005) 33.3 → 9.0; Phase III (2006–2009) 33.2 → 4.1.
  - Same directional drop for right and left adnexa. Supports the "same-case" adhesion-prevention standard as technique-driven, not tool-driven.
- Consider cross-specialty handoff criteria (robot, cardiothoracic for diaphragm, colorectal for bowel, urology) — decided intra-op when S-MAP surfaces disease beyond gyn scope [MC2; FACTS-LG surgery #5: diaphragm stripping with cardiothoracic].

### 4.4 Documentation Standard
- Still + video per station [MC2].
- Standardized station labels on pathology requisition so specimens are traceable to map position.
- Benefit claim (for methods justification): "Aids in staging & documentation — beneficial for research, education, & training" [AAGL slide 5].

## 5. Safety, Risk, and Informed Consent

- Increased operating time vs quick-look lap [MC2].
- Corresponding anesthesia-time risk and VTE risk from Trendelenburg duration [MC2].
- Patient counseling expectation: disclose time/anesthesia trade-off; two-stage surgery plan if advanced disease is found [MC2].
- Entry-technique safety baseline: Cochrane 57-study review, 9,865 participants, no mortality across entry techniques [fact-extracted-543].

## 6. Results Evidence (Case Series)

### 6.1 Single-Surgeon Detection Record (AAGL 2024)
- 4 rare cancers over 3 years, single non-oncologic MIGS surgeon, all cured [AAGL; FACTS 1, 10].
- 3 infertility patients in the series — 3/3 achieved subsequent pregnancy [AAGL].
- 3/4 = neuroendocrine tumor of appendix (incidence 0.95 / 100,000; probability of 3 by one surgeon ≈ 1 × 10⁻¹⁰) [AAGL; FACTS 2, 3].
- 1/4 = 2 mm well-differentiated papillary mesothelioma (WDPM) — otherwise only in case reports in the literature [AAGL; FACTS 4, 11].

### 6.2 Case Vignettes (JMIG-grade detail — use this version in the methods paper)

**Case A — 35 y/o G0** [JMIG expanded; AAGL abbreviated]
- **Presenting complaints:** dysmenorrhea, AUB, infertility, dyspareunia, incomplete bowel + bladder emptying, known fibroid uterus (largest 7.3 cm). No known asbestos exposure.
- **Index operation:** hysteroscopy + selective HSG with bilateral tubal cannulation + diagnostic laparoscopy using NCL + S-MAP.
- **Findings:** bilateral obstructed fallopian tubes with normal intratubal pressures after endosalpingolysis; endometrial + endocervical polyps; right fallopian tube externally compressed by leiomyomas.
- **Subsequent operation:** robotic-assisted myomectomy × 7 with bagged morcellation, laser excision of visible endometriosis, left paratubal cyst removal, right ovarian lesion removal.
- **Pathology:** 0.2 cm (= 2 mm) well-differentiated papillary mesothelioma (WDPM) on right pelvic sidewall; right ovarian lesion = **serous papillary fibroma** (rare incidental). **Visual endometriosis was pathologically negative** — i.e., her pain was not endo, it was WDPM + fibroids.
- **Disposition:** gyn-onc recommended CT every 3–6 months × 5 years, low recurrence risk. Pain, bowel, urinary symptoms all resolved post-op.
- **WDPM background for Introduction/Discussion:** only ~50 case reports total; genetically distinct from malignant mesothelioma; usually found in reproductive-age women incidentally at surgery (some present with pain, ascites, AUB); only some have asbestos history [JMIG refs 1–2: Shrestha 2020 PMID 32545767; Saha 2018 PMID 29692597].

**Case B — 27 y/o G0, second-opinion dysmenorrhea** [JMIG expanded]
- **Background:** prior endometriosis surgery at academic center including treatment of a 7 cm left ovarian endometrioma; ongoing severe dysmenorrhea; 1 cm recurrent endometrioma on US.
- **Index operation:** hysteroscopy + endometrial/endocervical cultures + biopsies + selective HSG + bilateral tubal cannulation + diagnostic laparoscopy with NCL/S-MAP + appendectomy.
- **Findings:** hysteroscopic endometritis, bilateral partial tubal occlusion (opened to normal), adhesions around L tube and L ovary to sidewall, extensive uterine serosal inflammation/adhesions, tubal endometriosis (proximal + distal), stage IV endometriosis, hemosiderin staining on appendix.
- **Pathology:** well-differentiated NET, WHO grade 1, 0.5 mm max microscopic dimension, free margins, confined to submucosa, 2 nodes negative — cured of appendiceal cancer per oncology.
- **Subsequent operation:** robotic-assisted laser endo surgery + left ovarian cystectomy + lysis of adhesions + adhesion prevention → dramatic dysmenorrhea improvement.

**Case C — 33 y/o G3P2012, PCOS + infertility** [JMIG expanded]
- **Presenting:** long irregular cycles, AUB, dysmenorrhea, continuous mucus discharge, infertility. Elevated 17-OHP up to **988 ng/dL**; non-classical CAH ruled out by endocrinology; diagnosed PCOS. Bilateral enlarged polycystic ovaries on US; LH:FSH reversal >2:1; elevated testosterone.
- **Index operation:** hysteroscopy + endometrial/endocervical cultures + biopsies + selective HSG + bilateral tubal cannulation + diagnostic laparoscopy with NCL/S-MAP + **robotic-assisted bilateral ovarian wedge resection** + laser endo surgery + appendectomy + cervical ectropion cautery + adhesion prevention.
- **Findings:** adenomyosis-suspicious uterus; endometriosis on bladder/uterus/broad ligaments/posterior cul-de-sac; L paratubal cyst; R ovarian cyst; enlarged polycystic ovaries bilaterally; bilateral partially obstructed tubes; appendiceal mass; grade III cervical ectropion.
- **Pathology:** Stage IV well-differentiated NET (grade 1) of appendix, mass 2.5 cm + 2 pelvic specimens positive (3 specimens 0.3–0.8 cm per path report).
- **Oncology follow-up:** hemicolectomy with **27 lymph nodes + margins + pelvis all negative**. No chemo/radiation indicated. 2-year surveillance → remission.
- **Pregnancy avoidance during surveillance:** patient used Creighton Model FertilityCare System + early luteal-phase progesterone monitoring ("family planning progesterone"). Reported efficacy: **0 pregnancies in 605 cycles ≈ 50 equivalent years** [JMIG ref 3: Hilgers 2020, *Linacre Q*, PMID 32431450].
- **Genetic incidental:** during surveillance, patient's mother (64) diagnosed with breast cancer → patient tested and found to carry **ATM gene mutation** (~25% lifetime breast cancer risk); now on q6-month MRI vs mammogram; planning pediatric genetic testing.
- **Teaching significance:** NCL/S-MAP indirectly triggered a cascade of multi-system care — PCOS management, stage IV endo excision, appendiceal NET cure, Creighton-gated fertility timing, hereditary cancer discovery.

**Case D — 27 y/o G1P1 ("LG")** [JMIG prose; expanded longitudinal detail in §6.4 from FACTS-LG]
- Recommended elsewhere for hysterectomy after outside diagnostic laparoscopy.
- Whittaker surgery #2 with NCL/S-MAP → grade 1 NET of appendix (1.5 cm), bilateral endometriomas, frozen pelvis, diaphragmatic endo, terminal ileum endo.
- Appendectomy + partial cecectomy curative for NET; patient chose observation over hemicolectomy.
- Surgery #3 (robotic laser excision + cystectomy + fimbrioplasty + ileocecectomy for ileocecal valve endo nodule + Gore-Tex + Seprafilm/steroid/LR slurry).
- Surgery #5 (6 months later, with cardiothoracic): diaphragm stripping + residual endo + R cystectomy + bilateral ovarian suspension + lysis.
- Post-op FSH 34.6 + DHEA → failed OI/trigger/TIC; grim fertility prognosis.
- Spontaneous conception within 1 year of last surgery (9 months post the FSH 34.6); **at JMIG-manuscript time, patient was in late third trimester**. Final outcome (healthy female at 36 weeks via C/S + severe inpatient PPD) is in [FACTS-LG] / §6.4, added after JMIG draft.

### 6.3 Inference
- Statistical argument: incidental detection rate far exceeds population base rate → technique (not chance) drives detection [JMIG/AAGL Discussion; FACTS-DB #3].
- Corollary: these cancers are likely more common than population registries suggest and routinely missed at quick-look laparoscopy [JMIG/AAGL].
- Population context for appendiceal NEN: incidence + survival data for England 1995–2018 in [JMIG ref 6: White et al. 2022, *Lancet Reg Health Eur* 23:100510, PMID **36176500**] — paper Naomi cites for "risk factors not reported in the literature" for endometriosis/infertility/common-exposure as NEN risk factors. **This confirms the open-question PMID from the previous pass.**

### 6.4 Expanded Case D Narrative ("LG", FACTS Elective) — *for case-report extension only*
*Drawn entirely from [FACTS-LG]. Use in the JRRM case-report extension and the RRM Academy teaching page; trim for any methods-paper version.*

- **Referral context:** 27 y/o G1P1 presented two days after an outside diagnostic laparoscopy (5/3/21). Outside surgeon identified bilateral hydrosalpinges, bilateral endometriomas (left ruptured intraoperatively, chocolate fluid), obliterated posterior cul-de-sac with bowel fused to posterior uterus, serosal uterine implants, posterior bladder implants, cecal adhesions with inflammatory changes. **Outside surgeon was unable to confidently identify the appendix** — later shown to contain an appendiceal mass on CT.
- **Outside recommendation:** hysterectomy + BSO with general surgery on standby for possible bowel resection. Patient declined in favor of fertility-sparing care.
- **Prior workup:** 3 IUIs on letrozole; pain worsened after IUIs. Spousal SFA (5/20): vol 1 mL, count 28 M/mL, motility 37%, total motile 10 M, 91% abnormal morphology. Normal colonoscopy Summer 2021.
- **Re-imaging:** ER CT (for post-op bilious emesis, 20 lb weight loss): "Appendix suspicious for appendicitis, periappendiceal inflammatory changes" — retrospectively consistent with occult appendiceal NET.
- **Surgery #2 (Whittaker, NCL + S-MAP):** appendiceal/cecal mass, completely obstructed right tube (recovered with cannulation), uterine septum (resected), bilateral endometriomas, frozen pelvis, extensive uterine adenomyotic implants, bladder involvement, right-sidewall disease, right-hemidiaphragm/falciform implants, terminal ileum implants, non-visualized posterior cul-de-sac.
- **Pathology (appendix + cecum):** well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumor, grade 1, 1.5 cm; margins uninvolved; foci suspicious for lymphovascular invasion (processing artifact not excluded). Cecal serosa: endometriosis. Endometrial biopsy: inactive endometrium, few plasma cells on CD138 immunostain — correlation recommended for possible chronic endometritis.
- **Oncology:** observation chosen over right hemicolectomy.
- **Surgery #3 (fertility-preserving reconstruction):** robotic-assisted laser excision of endometriosis, bilateral ovarian cystectomy, right fimbrioplasty, lysis of adhesions, excision of adenomyosis, robotic ileocecectomy/anastomosis (ileocecal valve nodule = endometriosis). Gore-Tex membrane implant + Seprafilm + steroid + LR slurry. Gore-Tex removed POD#10.
- **Surgery #5 (6 months later, combined cases):** diaphragm stripping with cardiothoracic surgery; residual endometriosis resection; right ovarian cystectomy; bilateral ovarian suspension; lysis of adhesions. Intraoperative slurry: Seprafilm + 300 mg solu-cortef + 300 mL LR.
- **Post-op reproductive course:** FSH 34.6 (prognosis called "grim"); DHEA supplementation; failed ovulation induction / trigger / TIC.
- **Outcome:** spontaneous conception with luteal-phase support within 1 year of last surgery (9 months after the FSH 34.6 draw). Pregnancy supported with progesterone. Delivered a healthy female infant at **36 weeks** via C-section (preterm labor + prior uterine surgery).
- **Postpartum:** severe PPD requiring inpatient admission despite IM progesterone support. Progesterone-in-pregnancy tracking (Pope Paul VI Institute reference curve, N=109) ran below the mean trajectory (see [FACTS-LG] chart, weeks 6–38).
- **Teaching significance:** this single case demonstrates the full NCL + S-MAP protocol value chain — occult appendiceal NET caught at index operation, fertility preserved, durable pregnancy achieved, with attention to upstream (CD138) and downstream (progesterone, PPD) RRM-integrated care.

## 7. Basic-vs-Advanced Comparison (Teaching Aid)

*Carry-over from Masterclass [MC2] — rendered as a table in the final piece. Trim to surgery-technique rows only for the methods paper version; keep full table for the RRM Academy teaching page.*

| Topic | Basic Approach | Advanced (NCL + S-MAP) |
|---|---|---|
| Laparoscopy | Quick look | Systematic map + close look / near contact |
| Appendix | May not look | Inspects; offers appendectomy if indicated |
| Diaphragm | Does not look | Inspects; possibly treats |
| Documentation | Minimal photos; obvious lesions only; few biopsies | Many photos/video; recognizes all appearances; many biopsies |
| Endometriomas | Drain or oophorectomy | Cystectomy; preserve ovary |
| Advanced disease | May recommend hysterectomy/menopause | Excises stage IV; fertility-preserving |
| Adhesion prevention | Not done | Integrated into index case |

## 8. Discussion

- Why standardization matters (reproducibility, multi-center data collection, training).
- Why NCL+S-MAP generalizes beyond endometriosis (non-gyn incidental pathology, e.g., appendiceal NET, WDPM).
- Boundary case: when to halt and bring in a subspecialist [MC2 two-surgery approach: colorectal, cardiothoracic, urologist].
- Limitations:
  - Single-surgeon case series; selection bias of second-opinion population.
  - No comparator group with quick-look lap (ethical/practical constraint).
  - WDPM incidence denominator is unknown (case-report-only literature).
- Call to action: "Scientific methods and standardization should be incorporated into the gold standard of endometriosis treatment." [AAGL Conclusion slide]

## 9. Conclusion

- NCL + S-MAP is a low-cost, reproducible protocol overlay on existing laparoscopic practice.
- Standardization is prerequisite to advancing endometriosis science and to catching non-gyn pathology like appendiceal NET/WDPM.
- Next steps: multi-surgeon adoption; registry of NCL/S-MAP cases; prospective comparison vs. quick-look standard.
