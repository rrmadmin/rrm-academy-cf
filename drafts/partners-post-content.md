The [Educational Partners program](/partners/) is a scope-bounded cohort of clinics that practice restorative reproductive medicine. The entry tier, called Friends, is open to clinics whose care aligns with the three RRM principles: find the cause, treat the disease, and restore function.

## What the Educational Partners Program Is

The Educational Partners program is a scope-bounded educational cohort, not a brand association. A clinic that joins is affirming clinical alignment, not marketing affiliation.

Three tiers structure the program. Friends is the entry level and is open for applications now. Partner and Accredited are in development. Neither is open yet, and no clinic carries either designation today.

## The Three RRM Principles as Qualifying Criteria

[The three principles of restorative reproductive medicine](/what-is-rrm/) form the clinical floor for partnership. They are not values statements. They describe how RRM clinicians approach every case.

### Find the cause

Evaluation does not stop at "normal" or "unexplained." [Cycle-based diagnostics (glossary)](/glossary/) give clinicians a data stream that standard workups often skip entirely: cycle-timed bloodwork, peak-day timing, luteal-phase hormones. The question driving every visit is specific: what is producing these symptoms?

### Treat the disease

The diagnosis determines the treatment. Hormonal support, surgical intervention, metabolic correction, or a combination: each matched to the finding. Nothing is empiric. Nothing is prescribed to mask a symptom while the underlying condition continues.

### Restore function

The measure of success is a body working as it should. Fertility, cycle regularity, hormonal balance, and pain resolution are outcomes of treating the disease. When the condition is treated, function often returns.

## The Clinical Scope Attestation

Every applicant affirms the same statement:

> Our care is delivered within the RRM clinical scope: treating diagnosable conditions with the goal of restoring healthy natural function, not suppressing symptoms or substituting for the body's own capacity.

This statement does real work. A practice whose first-line response to a cycle disorder is suppressive medication cannot honestly sign it. A practice that routes patients toward assisted reproduction before completing a diagnostic workup cannot honestly sign it either.

The scope is broader than fertility care. PMDD, pelvic pain, perimenopause, and teen cycle disorders all fall within it. Any practice whose clinical question is "what is wrong and how do we fix it" is working within scope. The attestation describes a clinical orientation, not a specialty.

## The Friends Tier and What Applying Gets You

Friends is the current entry tier. Applications are open. A clinic that applies and is approved becomes part of the RRM educational ecosystem: visible to patients and clinicians who look for practices aligned with cause-based care, and included in clinic-facing educational resources from RRM Academy.

Partner and Accredited tiers are in development. They are not yet open. No timeline is announced. No clinic holds either designation today.

Clinicians still building familiarity with the clinical tradition should start with [NaProTechnology](/naprotechnology/), which is the most structured and documented pathway within RRM. Fellowship training, surgical skill development, and cycle charting interpretation are all grounded there.

## Who This Is Not For

A practice that defaults to ovulation suppression as a first-line response to cycle disorders cannot honestly affirm the scope attestation. A practice that routes couples toward assisted reproduction before completing a diagnostic workup cannot either.

This is not a judgment. It is a statement of framework boundaries. A clinic operating in a different paradigm would be misrepresenting itself by applying. The attestation exists to prevent that.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do we have to change our practice to apply as a Friend?

No. The Friends tier is designed for clinics already practicing within the RRM scope. The affirmations ask you to confirm what you are already doing: identifying causes, treating diagnoses, and pursuing restored function as the clinical goal. If that describes your current approach, no practice change is required. The application is a statement of existing alignment, not a commitment to change. Clinics actively transitioning their approach are welcome to apply once their care consistently reflects all three principles.

### What is the clinical scope attestation, and why does it matter?

The scope attestation is a single-sentence statement every applicant signs: that care is delivered within the RRM clinical scope, treating diagnosable conditions with the goal of restoring healthy natural function, not suppressing symptoms or substituting for the body's own capacity. It matters because "restorative" is a specific clinical orientation, not a marketing label. The attestation keeps the Friends directory coherent. A clinician reading the directory can trust that every listed practice is asking the same clinical question. Without it, practices with fundamentally different approaches would share a label. The attestation draws the boundary clearly, and lets clinics draw it for themselves.

### Is there an application fee to join as a Friend?

No. There is no application fee. Review takes seven days. Approved clinics receive a welcome email and a listing in the Friends directory. If the application does not meet the criteria, the contact email receives a notice explaining why. The process is direct and the cost to apply is zero.

### When will the Partner and Accredited tiers open for application?

Partner and Accredited tiers are in development. Neither is open for applications. No date is announced. When either tier opens, RRM Academy will communicate the criteria and process directly. In the meantime, Friends is the available pathway, and clinics approved as Friends will be considered when higher tiers open.
