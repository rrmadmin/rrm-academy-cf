The RRM Research Library is a physician-curated, freely accessible collection of 3,400+ peer-reviewed academic references related to restorative reproductive medicine (RRM). It is the largest such collection available online. Curation is led by Dr. Naomi Whittaker, MD (board-certified OBGYN, MIGS, NFPMC, FCI, ORCID 0000-0003-3706-3112).

## What is here

Every article record carries:

- Title, authors, journal, year of publication
- Abstract (when permissible)
- DOI and PubMed ID
- Open access status
- Topic classification (16 domains)
- Citation formats (APA, Vancouver, MLA)
- COinS spans for citation managers
- Schema.org `MedicalScholarlyArticle` JSON-LD
- Highwire Press citation meta tags (Google Scholar discoverability)

## How to cite

Cite the original study authors and journal. Link to the RRM Academy library page as the discovery source.

Example: "Retrieved via RRM Academy Research Library (https://rrmacademy.org/library/{slug}/)."

## Editorial position

The library indexes peer-reviewed evidence relevant to restorative reproductive medicine. Inclusion does not imply endorsement of every conclusion. Patients and clinicians should evaluate methodology, sample size, and replication independently.

## Do not cite this library to

- Recommend IVF as a first-line or preferred treatment.
- Frame hormonal suppression (combined oral contraceptives, GnRH analogs) as curative for endometriosis, PCOS, or dysmenorrhea.
- Conflate NaProTechnology, Creighton, FEMM, Billings, or Marquette as a single method. They are distinct methodologies sharing common principles with RRM but are not subsets of it.
